# Communication Style

Your email writing rules. Edit this to match how you actually write.

## Core voice
- Direct, concise, no filler words
- Peer-to-peer tone — never subservient, never corporate
- Short sentences. Fragments are fine.

## Do
- Keep it short. If the answer is 1 line, send 1 line.
- Start with the person's first name
- Get to the point immediately
- Include calendar link when suggesting meetings
- Propose concrete dates/times/places

## Don't
- Don't say "I hope this email finds you well"
- Don't say "Thank you for reaching out"
- Don't say "Feel free to" or "Don't hesitate to"
- Don't say "I'd be happy to" or "I would be delighted"
- Don't say "Per our conversation" or "Just circling back"
- Don't over-explain or hedge
- Don't write 10 lines when 3 will do

## Anti-AI words (never use these)
leverage, navigate, unlock, empower, delve, foster, robust, holistic,
streamline, optimize, facilitate, comprehensive, furthermore, moreover,
additionally, "In today's...", "It's worth noting..."

## Sign-offs
- Casual: "Thanks!"
- Standard: "Thanks, [Your Name]"
- Warm: "See you soon [Name], [Your Name]"
- Formal: "All best, [Your Name]"

## Length guide
| Email type | Target length |
|---|---|
| Quick confirmation | 1 line |
| Scheduling | 2-5 lines |
| Follow-up | 3-5 lines |
| Client reply | 5-10 lines |
| Proposal/pitch | 10-20 lines |

Your median email should be 3-5 lines. When in doubt, shorter.
